# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three audiences share one screen, in very different contexts:

- **Rural citizens** of Suryapura — farmers, parents, students, elders. Often on
  a budget Android phone, a spotty 3G/4G signal, in bright outdoor daylight.
  Many read Devanagari more comfortably than Latin script; some rely on voice.
- **Local authorities & Panchayat members** — checking scheme status, posting
  notices, tracking infrastructure projects.
- **Development ambassadors** — the visible human face of the program, driving
  adoption and trust.

The job to be done: *"Show me, in my language, that my village is moving forward —
and give me one tap to act on the thing I came for"* (prices, a certificate, a
complaint, an emergency number).

## Product Purpose

A single, trustworthy front door to Suryapura's digital governance: weather and
mandi prices for farmers, school and literacy progress, live infrastructure
status, the Panchayat notice board, digital-identity shortcuts, and one-tap
emergency support. Success = a first-time visitor on a cheap phone understands
the village's progress and completes one real action within 30 seconds.

## Brand Personality

Rooted, dignified, optimistic. The voice of a respected village elder who has
learned the internet: warm, plain-spoken, never bureaucratic, never childish.
Three words: **earthbound, hopeful, credible.** It should feel like dawn over the
fields — the literal meaning of *Suryapura*, "city of the sun."

## Anti-references

- The generic "govtech" template: navy-blue header, stock handshake photo, dense
  link soup, English-only.
- AI-slop landing pages: glassmorphism on every surface, identical icon-card
  grids, gradient text, a hero-metric wall of big numbers.
- Saturated flag-palette literalism (kelly-green + traffic-orange). The Indian
  cultural reading should come from imagery, language and warmth — not a loud
  tricolor.
- Anything that reads as "designed in a city, for a city." It must feel like it
  belongs to the village.

## Design Principles

1. **Language is dignity.** Devanagari is first-class, not a translation
   afterthought. Every key label is bilingual.
2. **One thumb, one signal, one tap.** Critical actions are large, high-contrast,
   reachable, and survive a weak connection.
3. **Show the work, not the slogan.** Real progress bars, real prices, real
   notices — evidence over adjectives.
4. **Warmth from the soil, not the chrome.** Identity comes from photography,
   language and the green/gold palette — never from decorative effects.
5. **Fast on a bad day.** Clean DOM, system-cheap motion, optimized images. The
   page must feel premium on a 3G signal at noon.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Body text ≥ 4.5:1, large text ≥ 3:1, verified. Full keyboard
operability and visible focus rings. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored on every
animation. Touch targets ≥ 44px. Voice search affordance for low-literacy and
hands-busy users. Tested legibility in bright-daylight (high-contrast) conditions.
